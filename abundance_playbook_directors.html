01 — Overview
Brand & Story
Why Abundance exists, what we stand for, and the foundation everything else is built on.
Our positioning
"A spot where Taiwanese soulful cuisine meets Singapore's cravings."
Our story

Abundance was born from a simple belief — that Taiwanese cuisine deserved a proper home in Singapore. Not a hawker stall, not a mall chain, but a real space where people could experience the soul of Taiwan: bold flavours, generous portions, cold beer, and the kind of warmth that makes you want to stay a little longer.

We serve Taiwanese fusion cuisine alongside craft beer and cocktails — because great food is always better with a great drink. We didn't start Abundance to run a restaurant. We started it to create a place people genuinely love to be in.

What we stand for
Soulful food
Every dish should feel made with intention. Not just filling — memorable.
Great time, every time
The experience matters as much as the food. Guests leave happier than they arrived.
Consistency across outlets
RH, JB, or CS — the guest gets the same Abundance experience.
Scalable with soul
As we grow, we protect what makes us special. Scale doesn't mean generic.

05 — Leadership
P&L Framework
How to read the monthly P&L systematically. What each line means and who owns it.
Purpose: not to punish — to create visibility. When the Culinary Director sees food cost trending up, that's their problem to fix. The P&L makes ownership visible.
Reading the P&L — top to bottom
1. Revenue
Total vs last month and same month last year · Dine-in vs delivery split · Revenue per outlet — which is leading, which is lagging · Average spend per customer
2. Food Cost (COGS) — Owner: Culinary Director
Food cost % = COGS ÷ Revenue. Target ≤25% · Compare to last month — higher or lower, and why · Which categories driving cost: protein, beverages, packaging · Variance between invoiced cost and actual usage = wastage signal
3. Labour Cost — Owner: Operations Manager
Labour % = Payroll ÷ Revenue. Healthy F&B range: 25–35% · Track per outlet once payroll splits · Overstaffed on slow days, understaffed on peak days · Overtime: recurring or one-off?
4. Gross Profit
GP = Revenue − Food Cost − Labour Cost · Track GP % month on month · If GP is shrinking: is revenue down, food cost up, or labour up?
5. Operating Expenses
Rent — fixed, but right % of revenue? · Utilities — spikes? · Marketing spend — did it drive revenue? · Delivery platform fees and packaging
6. Net Profit
Net Profit = GP − Operating Expenses · SG casual dining benchmark: 10–15% healthy · below 8% needs attention · below 5% is a warning sign · Is it healthy enough to fund the next outlet?
06 — Leadership
Reset Conversation
The conversation to have with directors before introducing any new system. Have this first — the system has no foundation without it.
⚠ Do this before introducing new meeting structures or KPIs. Without this reset, nothing else lands.
Step-by-step script
1 — Open with growth context, not the problem
"I want to have an honest conversation today — not about a problem, but about where we're going. We're planning to scale Abundance from 3 outlets to more. That's exciting, but it means we all need to grow into our roles. What got us here won't get us there."
2 — Name what director-level means at the next stage
"At this stage, I need each of you to be the CEO of your function. Jaz — you own culinary, quality, and food cost end to end. Not just during R&D, but every month. Eddie — you own how the kitchen runs, the numbers, and the team. I need you both to come to me with insights and problems — not wait for me to find them."
3 — Name the gap, gently but directly
"Right now, our directors meeting ends up being mostly kitchen discussions. I need more from both of you — thinking beyond the next menu launch. Food cost trends, team performance, what's working and what's not. I haven't made this expectation clear enough before, and that's on me. But I'm making it clear now."
4 — Invite them in, don't just impose
"What would help you step into this? What do you need from me to think more strategically about your area? I'm not here to pile more on you. I want to build this together."
5 — Close with the new structure
"Starting next week, I want to change how we run our directors meeting. Each of us will come prepared with our numbers, one problem we're facing, and one thing we're planning ahead. And once a month, we'll review the P&L together — I'll walk you through how to read it."
Watch out for
  • Jaz may go quiet — he processes internally. Give him space after the conversation
  • Eddie may agree in the room but struggle to follow through — the new meeting structure will surface this quickly
  • Don't let the conversation drift into kitchen topics — keep redirecting to the bigger picture
  • If either gets defensive, acknowledge their contribution first before re-anchoring to expectations
