Standards
Service Standards
The baseline for how every Abundance guest should be served — every table, every shift.
Consistency is everything. A guest who visits RH, JB, and CS should get the same Abundance experience at all three. Your job is to deliver that standard every single time.
Table service flow
1
Greet within 60 seconds
Acknowledge guests as soon as they sit. Make eye contact, smile, and let them know you'll be right with them.
2
Take drinks order first
Always open with drinks before food. This is your first upselling opportunity. Recommend a craft beer, cocktail, or signature beverage.
3
Take food order and upsell
Know the menu well enough to make recommendations. Highlight seasonal items and house specials. Let the guest feel guided, not sold to.
4
Check back after food arrives
Within 2 minutes of food landing — check that everything is correct and the guest is happy. Fix any issues immediately.
5
Offer dessert before clearing
Before clearing the main course, offer a dessert. This is your second upselling moment. Know the dessert options and be ready to describe them.
6
Bill and farewell
Bring the bill promptly when requested. Thank the guest genuinely as they leave — not robotically.
Do's and don'ts
Do
  • Make eye contact and smile
  • Know the menu inside out
  • Upsell on every table
  • Apologise sincerely when things go wrong
  • Follow the food return guidelines
  • Keep the floor clean and tables cleared
  • Report issues to your manager immediately
Don't
  • Ignore guests or avoid eye contact
  • Apply discounts not on the approved list
  • Make promises you can't keep
  • Argue with guests — escalate to manager
  • Use your phone on the floor during service
  • Handle food complaints without following SOP
  • Leave dirty tables uncleared during service
Revenue
Upselling SOP
Upselling at Abundance is not optional — it is built into our service standard. Every table, every shift.
Targets: ≥80% of customers order at least 1 drink · ≥75% of transactions include at least 1 dessert. These numbers are tracked by management monthly.
Why upselling matters
A table that orders food, drinks, and dessert has a significantly higher average spend. This directly impacts outlet revenue and everyone's job security. Upselling isn't pushy — it's hospitality. You're helping guests discover things they'll enjoy.
Drinks — open with this
When taking the order, lead with drinks:
"Can I start you off with something to drink? We have our craft beers, signature cocktails, and some really good [seasonal/house recommendation] if you'd like to try something different."
If they decline: "No problem at all — would you like a soft drink or water while you look at the menu?"
Desserts — close with this
Before clearing mains, offer dessert:
"How was everything? Can I interest you in a dessert? Our [recommend 1–2 items] are really popular — a great way to end the meal."
Never ask "do you want dessert?" — always frame it as a recommendation.
Tips for effective upselling
  • Know the menu — you can't recommend what you don't know
  • Be genuine — recommend things you've tasted and actually like
  • Read the table — a couple on a date vs a family with kids need different approaches
  • Don't rush — let guests feel comfortable, not pressured
  • If declined once, don't push again for the same item

Menu
Food & Product Knowledge
Know what you're serving. Guests trust your recommendations — make them count.
Every new staff member must complete the food knowledge training within 2 months of joining. Your manager tracks this in the Product Knowledge Tracker.
How to build your food knowledge
1
Complete onboarding training
Start with the Abundance Training document — covers the basics of our brand, service, and menu.
2
Attend food tastings
Your manager will arrange food tasting sessions. Come ready to eat and pay attention to flavours, textures, and how to describe the dish to guests.
3
Study the Product Knowledge deck
Available in English and Chinese. Shows photos, key flavour notes, and talking points for each dish.
4
Apply it on the floor
The best training is doing it. Every guest question is a chance to practise your product knowledge.
When describing a dish to a guest
  • Lead with the key flavour — salty, rich, tangy, refreshing, light
  • Mention the texture — crispy, tender, silky, hearty
  • Note any dietary considerations if relevant — spicy, contains nuts, etc.
  • End with a personal touch — "It's one of our most popular dishes" or "I personally love this one"

Discounts Policy
Only approved discounts may be applied. No exceptions. When in doubt, ask your manager.
Hard rule: Never apply a discount that is not on the approved Discounts Summary list. No double discounts. Minimum spend requirements must be met before any discount is applied. Misuse of discounts is a disciplinary matter.
  • Only discounts listed in the Discounts Summary are valid
  • One discount per transaction — no stacking unless explicitly stated
  • Always check minimum spend before applying
  • If a guest claims a discount you're not sure about, check with your manager first
  • Never promise a discount that hasn't been pre-approved

Guest issues
Food Return Guidelines
What to do when a guest has a complaint about their food. Stay calm, follow the steps.
Do not make promises or offer refunds/replacements without following these guidelines and checking with your manager. A genuine apology goes a long way before any solution is offered.
When a guest complains about food
1
Listen and acknowledge
Let the guest finish. Do not interrupt or get defensive. Say: "I'm so sorry to hear that, let me look into this for you right away."
2
Assess the situation
Is this a genuine food issue (wrong item, foreign object, off taste) or a preference issue? Note exactly what the complaint is.
3
Inform your manager immediately
Never handle a food complaint alone. Bring your manager in. Do not offer refunds, replacements, or comps without manager approval.
4
Follow the guidelines
Your manager will handle the resolution using the Food Return Guidelines. Your job is to stay calm, be present, and make the guest feel heard.
5
Document it
After the guest leaves, ensure the incident is recorded. Your manager will handle this — just make sure it doesn't get forgotten.

Operations
Delivery Packing
How to pack delivery orders correctly and avoid disputes.
Every delivery dispute costs the business money. The best way to prevent disputes is to pack correctly every single time.
Packing checklist
  • Check order on screen before packing — match every item
  • Pack sauces and condiments in a separate container, clearly labelled
  • Seal all containers before placing in bag
  • Double-check the full order against the receipt before closing bag
  • Hand to rider only after bag is sealed and complete
  • Do not rush packing — a missed item always costs more time to fix
  • Do not include cutlery unless requested — it is optional and complimentary
If there's a packing error
  • Inform your manager immediately — don't wait for the dispute to come in
  • Note the order number, what was wrong, and what happened
  • If the rider is still present, correct the order before they leave if possible
  • Your manager will handle the dispute submission
Technical
WiFi Issues
What to do if the WiFi goes down at your outlet.
WiFi issues affect POS, delivery platforms, and payments. Act fast and follow the contingency guide.
  • Your first step is always to check the WiFi Contingency guide for your outlet
  • Try the standard restart steps listed in the guide before escalating
  • If the issue persists after following the guide, inform your manager immediately
  • Do not attempt to fix hardware or reconfigure router settings yourself
